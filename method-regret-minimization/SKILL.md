# Regret Minimization Framework (遗憾最小化框架)

**Origin:** Jeff Bezos, describing his decision to leave a Wall Street job to start Amazon (1994)

## Core Principle

> **Project yourself to age 80 and look back on your life. Which decision would you most regret NOT making?** Minimize the number of those regrets.

## The Framework

1. **Define the decision** — What choice are you facing?
2. **Project forward** — Imagine yourself at 80 years old, looking back
3. **Ask two questions**:
   - "Will I regret NOT doing this?"
   - "Will I regret DOING this?"
4. **Compare regrets** — Which regret is heavier?
5. **Choose** — Opt for the path that minimizes long-term regret

## Regret-Weighting Matrix

| | Regret if I DON'T | Regret if I DO |
|---|------------------|----------------|
| **Intensity** | How deeply would I regret it? (1–10) | How deeply would it sting? (1–10) |
| **Duration** | How long would the regret last? | How long? |
| **Reversibility** | Can I change my mind later? | Can I undo the consequences? |

## Decision Patterns

| Scenario | Typical Result |
|----------|---------------|
| **Quit safe job to start something** | Regret of NOT doing usually dominates |
| **Take financial risk for growth** | Regret of playing it safe dominates over time |
| **Have the hard conversation** | Regret of silence > regret of awkwardness |
| **Spend money on experiences** | Regret of missing life > regret of spending |
| **Bet everything on one idea** | Regret of failing is real — weigh both sides |

## Why Most People Regret Inaction More

Studies of the dying and elderly consistently find:
> **People regret the things they DIDN'T do far more than the things they DID.**

Top regrets of the dying (Bronnie Ware, palliative nurse):
1. I wish I'd had the courage to live a life true to myself, not the life others expected
2. I wish I hadn't worked so hard
3. I wish I'd had the courage to express my feelings
4. I wish I'd stayed in touch with my friends
5. I wish I'd let myself be happier

## Anti-Patterns

- ❌ **Ignoring actual consequences** — "I won't regret skydiving without training" — you might, for the remaining 50 years in a wheelchair
- ❌ **Only minimizing action-regret** — Some things you *will* regret doing. Don't dismiss those.
- ❌ **Regret-minimization as impulsiveness** — The framework says "think long-term," not "YOLO everything"
- ❌ **Never satisfied** — You can regret both paths. That means it's a hard decision; make it and move forward.

## When to Use

- Major career decisions (should I quit? take a risk? start a company?)
- Relationship decisions (have the hard conversation? make the move?)
- Relocation decisions (move to a new city/country?)
- "Golden handcuffs" situations — high pay, low fulfillment
- Any decision where the *safe* choice feels like slow regret
