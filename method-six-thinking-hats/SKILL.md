# Six Thinking Hats (六顶思考帽)

**Origin:** Edward de Bono (1985)

**Core idea:** Separate thinking into six distinct modes. Everyone "wears the same hat" at the same time — separating ego from thinking.

## The Six Hats

### White Hat — Facts & Data
- What information do we have?
- What information is missing?
- How do we get it?

### Red Hat — Emotions & Intuition
- What does my gut say?
- No need to justify feelings
- Short ~30 seconds per person

### Black Hat — Caution & Risks
- What could go wrong?
- Why might this not work?
- Potential pitfalls

### Yellow Hat — Optimism & Benefits
- What are the positives?
- What value could this create?
- Why is this worth doing?

### Green Hat — Creativity & Ideas
- What alternatives exist?
- How could we do this differently?
- No judgment during this hat

### Blue Hat — Process Control
- Which hat should we use next?
- Summarize what's been said
- Action plan and next steps

## Meeting Flow Example

| Step | Hat | Duration |
|------|-----|----------|
| Setup | Blue | 3 min |
| Facts | White | 10 min |
| Ideas | Green | 15 min |
| Risks | Black | 10 min |
| Benefits | Yellow | 10 min |
| Gut feel | Red | 3 min |
| Conclusion | Blue | 5 min |

## When to Use
- Team decisions with conflicting opinions
- Avoiding groupthink
- Making sure all perspectives are heard
- Structuring meetings for productivity
