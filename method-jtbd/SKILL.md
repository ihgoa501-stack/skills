# Jobs to Be Done (JTBD)

**Origin:** Clayton Christensen, Harvard Business School — "People don't buy products; they hire them to do a job."

## Core Concept

> **Customers don't buy a quarter-inch drill. They buy a quarter-inch hole.**

The "job" is the progress a person is trying to make in a particular circumstance — independent of your solution.

## The JTBD Framework

| Component | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| **Job** | What is the person trying to accomplish? |
| **Circumstance** | When and where does this job arise? |
| **Current solution** | What are they doing today to get the job done? |
| **Struggle** | What's hard/frustrating about the current solution? |
| **Success criteria** | How do they know the job is done well? |

## Job Statement Format

> **When** [situation/trigger], **I want to** [motivation/functional job], **so I can** [expected outcome/emotional job].

Example: *"When I'm about to host a dinner party and realize I know nothing about wine, I want to quickly find a bottle that pairs with the menu, so I can feel confident and not embarrass myself."*

## Types of Jobs

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Functional** | The practical task to get done | "Transport me from A to B" |
| **Emotional** | How they want to feel | "Feel in control and safe" |
| **Social** | How they're perceived by others | "Look environmentally conscious" |

## How to Discover Jobs

1. **Interview switchers** — Talk to people who recently switched from one solution to another
2. **Look for workarounds** — When people jury-rig solutions using duct tape + Excel + a prayer
3. **Non-consumption** — Who's not using anything because no solution fits their circumstance?
4. **Competing solutions aren't just your competitors** — For a restaurant, competitors include: other restaurants, cooking at home, grocery delivery, skipping dinner

## Anti-Patterns

- ❌ **"The job is to use our product"** — The job exists whether your product does or not
- ❌ **Defining the job by features** — "They want a faster database" is a feature; "They want answers before their coffee gets cold" is a job
- ❌ **Ignoring the circumstance** — The same person has different jobs at different times. Context is everything.

## When to Use

- Product discovery — before writing any code, define the job
- Competitor analysis — competitors are anything that gets the job done, not just similar products
- Pricing — price based on the value of getting the job done, not cost-plus
- Positioning — marketing that speaks to the job, not the feature list
