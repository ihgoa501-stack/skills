# Golden Circle / 黄金圈法则 (Why → How → What)

**Origin:** Simon Sinek, *Start with Why* (2009)

**Core idea:** Great leaders and organizations think from the **inside out** — they start with **Why** (purpose), then **How** (method), then **What** (result). Most people and companies communicate from the outside in.

```
         ┌─────────────┐
         │   What      │  ← 做什么（表象）
         │ ┌─────────┐ │
         │ │  How    │ │  ← 怎么做（方法）
         │ │┌───────┐│ │
         │ ││ Why  ││ │ │  ← 为什么（本质）
         │ │└───────┘│ │
         │ └─────────┘ │
         └─────────────┘
```

## The Three Layers

| Layer | Meaning | Question | Level |
|-------|---------|----------|-------|
| 🔴 **Why** | Purpose / Cause / Belief | Why do we exist? Why does this matter? | **Core** — strategy, motivation |
| 🟡 **How** | Process / Method | How do we do it? What's our approach? | **Middle** — tactics, differentiation |
| 🔵 **What** | Product / Result | What do we do? What's the output? | **Surface** — results, evidence |

## Two Thinking Paths

### Most people (outside → in)
```
What → How → Why
```
They know what they do. Some know how. Few ask why.

### Inspired leaders (inside → out)
```
Why → How → What
```
They start with purpose, then find the method, then the result follows.

## Classic Example: Apple

| | Typical pitch | Golden Circle pitch |
|---|--------------|-------------------|
| Why | — | "Everything we do challenges the status quo" |
| How | "Beautiful design, easy to use" | "We make beautifully designed products" |
| What | "Buy our computers and phones" | "We happen to make great computers" |

**Result:** People don't buy what you do — they buy **why** you do it.

## Application Scenarios

| Scenario | Use |
|----------|-----|
| Brand / marketing | Clarify core message — lead with Why |
| Team motivation | Explain purpose before tasks — people need to believe |
| Personal career | "Why am I doing this?" — find intrinsic drive |
| Decision making | Filter options by alignment with Why |
| Presentation | Structure: Why → How → What (most persuasive order) |

## Quick Practice

To think in Golden Circle, ask in order:

1. **Why** — Why does this work matter? What would be lost if it didn't exist?
2. **How** — What principles guide our approach? What makes our method unique?
3. **What** — What specifically will we do or produce?

## When to Use
- Crafting a brand, mission, or value proposition
- Giving a persuasive presentation or pitch
- Motivating a team — people need purpose, not just tasks
- Making strategic decisions — filter by alignment with core Why
- Personal reflection — clarifying your own motivations

## When NOT to Use
- Pure operational execution (just need to get it done)
- The "Why" is already clear to everyone and well-understood
- Factual / technical communication that should stay objective
