# Eat the Frog (吃青蛙)

**Origin:** Brian Tracy, *Eat That Frog!* (2001) — inspired by Mark Twain

> "If the first thing you do each morning is to eat a live frog, you can go through the day with the satisfaction of knowing that that is probably the worst thing that is going to happen to you all day." — Mark Twain

**Core idea:** Identify your biggest, most important, most procrastinated-on task — and do it **first**, before anything else.

## The Method

1. **Identify your frog** — the one task that will make the biggest difference but you're most tempted to avoid
2. **Do it first** — before checking email, before meetings, before anything easy
3. **Nothing else until the frog is eaten** — protect this block of time

## Why It Works
- Willpower is highest in the morning
- Momentum — completing the hardest task builds confidence
- Once it's done, everything else feels easy by comparison
- No dread lingering over the rest of the day

## Tips
- **Plan the frog the night before** — know what you're doing before you sit down
- **2-minute rule:** If the frog takes <2 min, eat it immediately
- **Eat multiple frogs:** If your frogs are small, eat a few. If one is massive, eat just part of it (but the ugliest part)

## When to Use
- You're procrastinating on something important
- Your day gets derailed by busywork before you tackle real work
- You feel overwhelmed and don't know where to start
