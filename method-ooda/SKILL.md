# OODA Loop

**Origin:** Colonel John Boyd, US Air Force (fighter pilot strategy)

**Core idea:** Speed through the Observe → Orient → Decide → Act cycle faster than your opponent to disrupt their decision-making.

## The Cycle

```
    Observe
       ↓
    Orient  ←──── Feedback
       ↓
    Decide
       ↓
    Act ──────────→ (disrupts opponent's OODA)
```

| Phase | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Observe** | Gather information from environment, reports, sensors |
| **Orient** | Analyze — mental models, experience, culture, new info — most critical phase |
| **Decide** | Choose a course of action from options |
| **Act** | Execute, observe the result, repeat |

> **Boyd's insight:** The side that completes the OODA loop fastest gets inside the opponent's decision cycle, causing confusion and paralysis.

## Applied to Business

| Phase | Questions |
|-------|-----------|
| Observe | What's changing in the market? What are competitors doing? What do users say? |
| Orient | What does this mean given our strategy, capabilities, and constraints? |
| Decide | Which initiative do we prioritize? What do we stop doing? |
| Act | Ship the feature, cut the price, pivot the roadmap |

## Improving Your OODA Speed
- Reduce decision latency (delegate, pre-commit)
- Improve feedback loops (ship fast, measure, learn)
- Expand your orientation domain (diverse inputs, multiple mental models)
- Practice (deliberate repetition under time pressure)

## When to Use
- Competitive situations requiring fast adaptation
- Crisis management and rapid response
- Any environment with a fast-changing opponent
