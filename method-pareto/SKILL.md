# Pareto Principle (二八法则 / 80/20 Rule)

**Origin:** Vilfredo Pareto (Italian economist, 1906) — observed 80% of land in Italy was owned by 20% of the population

**Core idea:** A minority of inputs produces the majority of outputs. Identify and focus on that 20%.

## Common Patterns

| Domain | 80% of Results (Effect) | 20% of Effort (Cause) |
|--------|------------------------|----------------------|
| Business | Revenue | Top customers |
| Software | User complaints | Most common bugs |
| Product | Usage | Core features |
| Time management | Value produced | Key tasks |
| Code | Execution time | Hot paths |
| Learning | Useful knowledge | Foundational concepts |

## How to Apply

1. **List** all the inputs / causes / efforts
2. **Measure** which ones produce the most output
3. **Focus** on the top 20%
4. **Cut or minimize** the bottom 80% of low-impact items

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Taking the ratio literally (it's a heuristic, not 80% exactly)
- ❌ Ignoring the long tail entirely (sometimes it matters)
- ❌ Applying once and forgetting — the 20% shifts over time

## When to Use
- Overwhelmed with too many priorities
- Resource-constrained and need leverage
- Analyzing what to cut or what to double down on
