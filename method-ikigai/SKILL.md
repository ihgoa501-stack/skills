# Ikigai — Reason for Being (生き甲斐 / 人生意义)

**Origin:** Japanese philosophy from Okinawa — one of the world's "Blue Zones" where people live longest. *Iki* (生命) = life, *gai* (甲斐) = worth/value.

## The Four Circles

```
            What You
              Love
               /\
              /  \
             /    \
            /      \
           /        \
   What   / Ikigai   \  What You're
  World  /            \  Good At
  Needs  \            /
           \        /
            \      /
             \    /
              \  /
               \/
         What You Can
          Be Paid For
```

| Circle | Question |
|--------|----------|
| **What you love** | What activities make you lose track of time? |
| **What you're good at** | What skills do people seek your help for? |
| **What the world needs** | What problems make you angry enough to solve? |
| **What you can be paid for** | What value can someone exchange money for? |

## The Four Intersections

| Intersection | Result | Feeling |
|-------------|--------|---------|
| Love + Good At | **Passion** | Joy, but no security |
| Love + World Needs | **Mission** | Purpose, but may not pay |
| Good At + Paid For | **Profession** | Comfort, but emptiness |
| Paid For + World Needs | **Vocation** | Usefulness, but may drain you |
| **All Four** | **Ikigai** | Deep fulfillment |

## How to Find Yours

1. **Start with what you love** — List the activities where time disappears
2. **Map your competence** — What do others consistently praise you for? What comes easily to you that's hard for others?
3. **Identify the need** — What problem in the world do you viscerally care about?
4. **Find the market** — Who pays for this? What's the economic model?
5. **Look for overlap** — Where do all four circles intersect? If the intersection is empty, can you expand one circle?

## The Okinawan Perspective

Western interpretations emphasize the four-circle diagram above. But in Okinawa, **ikigai is simpler**: it's the thing that gets you out of bed in the morning. It doesn't require all four circles — it can be gardening, cooking, community, a craft. The key is having a reason to wake up.

> **Your ikigai doesn't have to be your career.** It's your reason for being, which may or may not be what you're paid for.

## Anti-Patterns

- ❌ **Waiting to find it** — Ikigai is discovered through action, not introspection. Try things, adjust, try again.
- ❌ **Requiring all four circles** — Perfection is the enemy. Start with two overlapping and expand.
- ❌ **Confusing ikigai with ambition** — Ikigai can be small, quiet, and humble. It doesn't need to be a world-changing startup.
- ❌ **One-and-done** — Ikigai evolves as you age. What was ikigai at 25 may not be at 55.

## When to Use

- Career exploration — you have multiple paths, none feels "perfect"
- Mid-career crisis — the job pays well but feels empty (Profession without Mission)
- Major life decisions — should I stay on this path or change?
- Team / culture building — help people connect daily work to a sense of purpose
- Retirement planning — what will be your ikigai when the job ends?
