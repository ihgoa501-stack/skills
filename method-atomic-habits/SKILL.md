# Atomic Habits (原子习惯)

**Origin:** James Clear, *Atomic Habits* (2018)

Core idea: **1% improvement every day compounds to ~37× better over a year.**

## The Four Laws of Behavior Change

### Build a Good Habit

| Law | Formula | Example |
|-----|---------|---------|
| **1. Make it obvious** | Design environment cues | Put running shoes by the door |
| **2. Make it attractive** | Pair with something enjoyable | Listen to podcasts only while running |
| **3. Make it easy** | Reduce friction, 2-minute rule | "Put on workout clothes" = first step |
| **4. Make it satisfying** | Immediate reward | Mark a streak on the calendar |

### Break a Bad Habit (Inversion)

| Law | Formula | Example |
|-----|---------|---------|
| **1. Make it invisible** | Remove cues | Keep phone in another room |
| **2. Make it unattractive** | Reframe mindset | "Scrolling is wasting my potential" |
| **3. Make it difficult** | Increase friction | Log out of social media after each use |
| **4. Make it unsatisfying** | Add immediate cost | Accountability partner penalty |

## Key Concepts

### Habit Stacking
> "After [current habit], I will [new habit]."

Examples:
- "After I pour my morning coffee, I will meditate for 60 seconds."
- "After I sit down at my desk, I will write one sentence."

### The 2-Minute Rule
Scale down any habit so it takes ≤2 minutes to start:
- "Read for 30 minutes" → "Read one page"
- "Study for 2 hours" → "Open my notes"
- "Run 5 km" → "Put on running shoes"

### Identity-Based Habits
- **Outcome-based:** "I want to run a marathon" (results)
- **Identity-based:** "I am a runner" (identity)

Focus on the **type of person you want to become**, not just the outcome.
