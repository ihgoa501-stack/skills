# Circle of Competence (能力圈)

**Origin:** Popularized by Warren Buffett and Charlie Munger — inspired by Thomas Watson Sr. (IBM founder)

## Core Principle

> **Knowing what you don't know is more important than knowing what you do know.**

Your Circle of Competence is the domain where your knowledge and judgment are deeply reliable. Outside it, you're guessing — and you may not even realize how badly.

## The Three Circles

```
  ┌──────────────────────────────┐
  │    What You Think You Know   │  ← Dangerous: overconfidence zone
  │  ┌──────────────────────┐    │
  │  │  What You Actually   │    │
  │  │       Know           │    │  ← Circle of Competence
  │  └──────────────────────┘    │
  │                              │
  └──────────────────────────────┘
       Outside = What You Know You Don't Know  ← Humble, safe
```

## How to Apply

1. **Define your circle** — What domains have you earned expertise in? Not "interested in" but "demonstrated skill in"
2. **Operate inside it** — For high-stakes decisions, stay within the circle
3. **Expand slowly** — Read, practice, make small bets to grow the boundary
4. **Recognize the edge** — When you're near the boundary, slow down and seek expertise
5. **Say no outside it** — "I don't know" is a strength, not a weakness

## Signals You're Outside Your Circle

- You can't explain the idea to a smart 12-year-old
- You're using jargon you don't fully understand
- Your confidence comes from reading one article
- You can't answer "what would prove me wrong?"
- You're investing/promising/committing based on someone else's certainty

## Practical Examples

| Domain | Inside Circle | Outside Circle |
|--------|--------------|----------------|
| Investing | Your industry's companies | Hot crypto token everyone's talking about |
| Tech | Your stack's performance tuning | "Let's rewrite in Rust" (never used it) |
| Career | Your craft and network | "I could run a restaurant" (never worked in one) |

## Anti-Patterns

- ❌ **Circle creep** — "I read 3 articles, I'm an expert now"
- ❌ **Never leaving the circle** — Growth requires stepping just outside it intentionally
- ❌ **Confusing interest with competence** — loving music ≠ being a record producer
- ❌ **Expert-in-everything** — no one has a circle that covers all domains

## When to Use

- Making career decisions — are you betting on your competence or on a story?
- Evaluating investment advice — is the source inside their circle?
- Deciding whether to build vs. buy vs. hire
- Hiring — assessing whether a candidate knows their own boundaries
