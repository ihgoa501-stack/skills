# AARRR Pirate Metrics (海盗指标)

**Origin:** Dave McClure, 500 Startups (2007) — pronounced "AARRR!" like a pirate

## The Five Stages

| Stage | Question | Key Metric |
|-------|----------|------------|
| **Acquisition** | How do users find you? | Traffic sources, CPC, signups/channel |
| **Activation** | Is the first experience great? | % signups that hit "aha moment" |
| **Retention** | Do they come back? | DAU/MAU, churn rate, week-1 retention |
| **Revenue** | How do you make money? | ARPU, LTV, conversion to paid |
| **Referral** | Do they tell others? | NPS, viral coefficient, referral rate |

## How to Use

1. **Map your funnel** — What does each stage look like for your product?
2. **Measure each stage** — You can't fix what you can't see
3. **Find the bottleneck** — Which stage leaks the most? Fix that first
4. **Optimize systematically** — Don't jump around; one stage at a time
5. **Re-measure** — Did the bottleneck move? Now fix the next one

## The One Metric That Matters (at each stage)

| Stage | If this is your bottleneck, focus on: |
|-------|--------------------------------------|
| Acquisition | SEO / Ads / Content / Partnerships |
| Activation | Onboarding UX / Tutorial / First-value speed |
| Retention | Notifications / Email / Habit loops / Product quality |
| Revenue | Pricing / Trial conversion / Upsell |
| Referral | Share prompts / Incentives / Virality mechanics |

## Anti-Patterns

- ❌ **Optimizing all stages at once** — You'll do all of them poorly. Pick the bottleneck.
- ❌ **Acquisition obsession** — Pouring users into a leaky bucket (bad activation/retention)
- ❌ **Measuring everything, understanding nothing** — 50 dashboards ≠ insights
- ❌ **Ignoring the qualitative** — Numbers tell you *what*; user interviews tell you *why*

## When to Use

- You have a product and don't know where to focus improvement
- Launching a new product and need a measurement framework
- Board/investors ask for growth metrics
- Your growth has plateaued and you need to find where the leak is
