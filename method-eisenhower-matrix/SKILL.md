# Eisenhower Matrix (四象限法则 / Time Management Quadrant)

**Origin:** Dwight D. Eisenhower → Stephen Covey, *The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People*

## The Matrix

| | **Urgent** | **Not Urgent** |
|---|-----------|----------------|
| **Important** | **I. Do Now** (crises, deadlines, firefighting) | **II. Schedule** (planning, relationships, exercise, deep work) |
| **Not Important** | **III. Delegate / Batch** (interruptions, busywork, some meetings) | **IV. Eliminate** (time wasters, trivia, excessive browsing) |

## How to Use

1. List all your tasks
2. Place each in one quadrant
3. Act accordingly:

| Quadrant | Action |
|----------|--------|
| I (Important + Urgent) | **Do it now** — limit time here |
| II (Important + Not Urgent) | **Schedule it** — this is where high-leverage work lives |
| III (Not Important + Urgent) | **Delegate or batch** — minimize |
| IV (Not Important + Not Urgent) | **Eliminate** — don't do it |

## Key Insight

**Spend most of your energy in Quadrant II** (important but not urgent). Neglecting it turns strategic work into Quadrant I crises over time. Examples of Quadrant II:
- Strategic planning
- Skill development / learning
- Relationship building
- Preventive maintenance
- Exercise and health

## Common Pitfalls
- ❌ Living in Quadrant I all day (burnout mode)
- ❌ Confusing "urgent" with "important"
- ❌ Feeling productive from clearing Quadrant III busywork
