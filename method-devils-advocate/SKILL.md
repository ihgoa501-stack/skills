# Devil's Advocate (魔鬼代言人)

**Origin:** Catholic Church canonization process (16th century) — the *Advocatus Diaboli* was appointed to argue *against* sainthood to ensure only the truly worthy were canonized.

## Core Idea

**Before committing to a decision, someone must play the enemy of the idea.** The Devil's Advocate role is to deliberately and systematically challenge a proposal, exposing weak points that optimism and groupthink would otherwise hide.

## The Process

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1. **Define the proposal** | State the idea/decision clearly — what are we committing to? |
| 2. **Assign the role** | Designate one person (or yourself) as the Devil's Advocate |
| 3. **Build the case against** | Argue the opposite: why this will fail, what's wrong with it |
| 4. **Document challenges** | Record every objection, risk, and flaw surfaced |
| 5. **Respond & strengthen** | Address the challenges — revise the plan or confirm it holds up |
| 6. **Decide** | Make the call with full awareness of both sides |

## How to Play the Role

1. **Be specific, not negative** — "Revenue projection is wrong because X" > "This won't work"
2. **Argue the strongest counter-case**, not a straw man — make the opposition as intelligent as you are
3. **Stay impersonal** — attack the idea, never the person
4. **Know when to stop** — once challenges are surfaced and addressed, move on

## Key Questions to Ask

- What would have to be true for this to fail catastrophically?
- What's the weakest assumption we're making?
- If a competitor knew our plan, how would they exploit it?
- Who loses if this succeeds? What would they do in response?
- If we're wrong in 6 months, what will have caused it?
- What data would prove we shouldn't do this?

## Execution Formats

| Format | Best For |
|--------|----------|
| **Role-play meeting** — someone formally plays the adversary | Team decisions, strategy reviews |
| **Self-play** — argue against your own idea in writing | Solo decisions, proposal prep |
| **Red Team** — a separate group briefed to tear the plan apart | High-stakes decisions, security |
| **Pre-mortem** — assume the plan already failed, work backwards | Project kickoffs, new initiatives |

## Anti-Patterns

- ❌ Becoming the perpetual critic — constant negativity kills team morale
- ❌ Confusing Devil's Advocate with being correct — the goal is better decisions, not winning the argument
- ❌ Using weak objections — a bad counter-argument gives false confidence
- ❌ Never exiting the role — once concerns are surfaced and addressed, commit and move forward

## When to Use

- High-stakes decisions with significant downside
- Everyone in the room agrees too quickly (groupthink danger)
- Launching a new product, strategy, or major initiative
- You notice yourself only seeking evidence that supports your view (confirmation bias)
- Pre-mortem exercises before starting a project
